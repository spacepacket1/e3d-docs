# Story Reference

Use this page to decode story types quickly and understand the expected evidence pattern behind each one. It is the “what does this label mean?” companion to the Story Guide.

## How to use the catalog

When you see a new story type in the feed:

1. Read the title for the pattern.
2. Read the summary for the implication.
3. Inspect the evidence notes for the graph shape you should expect.
4. Open the relevant guide if you need deeper workflow context.
5. Ask whether the story is about a single event, a repeated pattern, or part of a broader campaign.

The goal is to move from label → meaning → proof as quickly as possible.

## Story families

E3D story types are grouped into a few broad families:

- **Rabbit forensics** — structure-first graph findings like loops, clusters, mirrors, and staging
- **Market/activity** — price, volume, liquidity, discovery, and rotation signals
- **Risk/confirmation** — manipulation, liquidity, or validation signals that help separate noise from signal

## Rabbit forensics stories

These stories are the most investigation-centric. They tend to describe graph shapes, wallet interactions, and structural relationships rather than just market outcomes.

### Rabbit Thesis

- **What it is:** A bundled intelligence brief that combines multiple rabbit findings into one narrative.
- **Why it matters:** Gives you the higher-level explanation once multiple rabbit patterns point at the same entity or time window.
- **Evidence shape:** Expect a synthesis of several graph patterns, often centered around the same token or wallet cluster.

### Rabbit Staging

- **What it is:** A readiness signal that detects wallets or subgraphs waking up before an on-chain action.
- **Why it matters:** Often the earliest sign that a larger move is being prepared.
- **Evidence shape:** Dormancy breaks, funding events, infra touchpoints, and short-horizon sequencing.

### Rabbit Funnel

- **What it is:** A convergence pattern where many sources route into fewer sinks.
- **Why it matters:** Useful for spotting consolidation, staging, collection points, or operational sinks.
- **Evidence shape:** Source → mid → sink paths, deduped sink components, and sparse explainable graphs.

### Rabbit Cluster

- **What it is:** A topology expansion around a seed that measures density, reciprocity, and hub structure.
- **Why it matters:** Reveals operational design such as coordination meshes or staging complexes.
- **Evidence shape:** Multi-depth neighborhoods and topology metrics.

### Rabbit Loop

- **What it is:** Circular or near-circular recycling paths within a bounded subgraph.
- **Why it matters:** Strong signal for obfuscation, wash-like behavior, liquidity recycling, or testing loops.
- **Evidence shape:** Cycle paths and supporting edges in a closed structure.

### Rabbit Mirror

- **What it is:** A group of wallets with similar counterparties, timing, or behavior.
- **Why it matters:** Detects coordination even when wallets never transact directly with each other.
- **Evidence shape:** Group-to-counterparty bipartite or shared-neighborhood patterns.

## Market and activity stories

These stories focus more on market behavior than on a single graph motif. They help you understand how capital is moving, what is gaining attention, and whether the move looks organic or manufactured.

### Surge

- **What it is:** A price surge paired with on-chain activity context.
- **Why it matters:** Separates real participation from thin-liquidity spikes.
- **Evidence shape:** Price delta plus activity and participation deltas.

### Whale Stealth Flow

- **What it is:** A large-holder movement story with obfuscation or stealth routing characteristics.
- **Why it matters:** Often an early positioning clue before the market fully reacts.
- **Evidence shape:** Large wallet flows, repeated counterparties, and meaningful net movement.

### Ecosystem Shift

- **What it is:** Cross-chain day-over-day volume rotation.
- **Why it matters:** Helps you see capital flowing between ecosystems rather than looking at one chain in isolation.
- **Evidence shape:** Aggregate chain-level statistics rather than a deep graph.

### Bridge Paths

- **What it is:** Cross-chain movement traces.
- **Why it matters:** Useful when you need to follow capital as it moves across chains and infrastructure.
- **Evidence shape:** Multi-hop bridge trails and destination topology.

### Discovery

- **What it is:** A story that surfaces new or unknown contracts with meaningful activity.
- **Why it matters:** Helps you find early opportunities before a token is broadly labeled.
- **Evidence shape:** Activity plus identity metadata, usually with graph drilldown available.

### Hot Links

- **What it is:** A token with unusually high transaction activity in a short window.
- **Why it matters:** Good for rapid triage when you want to know where attention is concentrating.
- **Evidence shape:** Token-centric activity burst with counterparty expansion.

### Category / sector stories

- **What it is:** Rotation across a token category or cohort.
- **Why it matters:** Gives a sector lens that is hard to extract from a single token chart.
- **Evidence shape:** Cohort lists, leaders, and relative momentum.

## Risk and confirmation stories

These stories help you decide whether the structure supports the move or warns you away from it.

### Liquidity Drain

- **What it is:** A pool TVL drop that is happening faster than price has fully reflected.
- **Why it matters:** Often an early warning for rug risk, slippage deterioration, or market-maker exit.
- **Evidence shape:** TVL snapshots, drop percentages, and pool history.

### Exchange Flow

- **What it is:** Net flow into or out of exchange wallets for a token.
- **Why it matters:** Exchange inflows can imply sell pressure; outflows can imply accumulation or treasury movement.
- **Evidence shape:** Net flow direction and magnitude over the lookback window.

### Momentum Divergence

- **What it is:** Price moving one way while fundamentals move the other.
- **Why it matters:** Classic warning sign that a move may be losing support.
- **Evidence shape:** Price, user count, volume, and TVL comparison across matched windows.

### Wash Trade

- **What it is:** Artificial volume patterns that look like round-tripping or recycling.
- **Why it matters:** Lets you discount fake demand and avoid mistaking manufactured liquidity for real interest.
- **Evidence shape:** Sender/receiver repetition, round-trip ratios, and inflated volume metrics.

### Concentration Shift

- **What it is:** Fast changes in holder concentration, especially top-wallet share.
- **Why it matters:** Useful for identifying building, distribution, or control shifts.
- **Evidence shape:** Concentration deltas and the wallets driving them.

### Token Quality Score

- **What it is:** A composite ranking for new or newly surfaced tokens.
- **Why it matters:** First-pass filter for analyst attention.
- **Evidence shape:** Score breakdown across liquidity, distribution, security, sell health, and stability.

### Smart Money

- **What it is:** Tokens being accumulated by historically profitable wallets.
- **Why it matters:** Helps you find candidate trades by watching quality investor behavior.
- **Evidence shape:** Buyer wallet list, win rate, historical return, and token context.

### Stealth Accumulation

- **What it is:** Many small buys adding up to a coordinated position.
- **Why it matters:** Helps spot whales splitting size across wallets to stay hidden.
- **Evidence shape:** Aggregate USD amount, active buyer count, and size ceilings.

### Insider Timing

- **What it is:** Pre-event buying around major announcements or catalysts.
- **Why it matters:** Useful for building a candidate insider list or monitoring early positioning.
- **Evidence shape:** Pre-event buying compared to baseline windows.

### Volume Profile Anomaly

- **What it is:** A shift in when and how a token trades, including odd hour-of-day patterns.
- **Why it matters:** Can signal a new participant base or OTC-like distribution.
- **Evidence shape:** Histogram shifts, KL divergence, size clustering, and dominant buckets.

### Spread Widening

- **What it is:** Expected slippage worsens faster than trading activity would justify.
- **Why it matters:** Direct market-depth warning that liquidity is thinning.
- **Evidence shape:** Current vs prior TVL, slippage ratio, trade activity, and median trade size.

## How to read the catalog in practice

When a story appears, ask three questions:

1. **Is this a structure story, a market story, or a risk story?**
2. **What evidence shape should I expect if the claim is real?**
3. **What follow-up view should I open next: stories, /links, token view, or a thesis?**

That sequence keeps you from over-reading a headline and pushes you back toward evidence.

## Why it matters

The reference page is the quick decoder ring for the story feed. It helps you understand what the system is telling you before you jump into deeper investigation.
